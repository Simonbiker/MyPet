Walking in hot weather

Before walking your dog check the tempture of the bavement. You can do this by placing the palm of your hand on the concrete. If you are unable to hold there then your dog should not be walking.

  1. Make sure your dog always has access to fresh clean water.
  2. Make sure your dog has access to shade or cooler rooms. Pull down blinds or draw curtains if necessary.
  3. As a cooling treat, you can give your dog a handful of frozen peas. You can also add ice to their water or freeze their favourite treat and give it as an ice-cream.
  4. If you want to walk your dog, try to do so in the early morning and evenings. If you are walking your dog on concrete, touch the concrete with your palm as a test. If it feels hot or you can't keep you hand comfortably on the concrete for 5 seconds, it is too hot to walk your dog with their sensitive pads.
  5. If you do walk you dog, make sure you carry water for them.
  6. Never leave your dog in the car even with the windows open, this can prove fatal if the car overheats. This can happen in less than 5 minutes.
  7. Be extra vigilant with older or overweight dogs as they are more prone to overheating.Â 
  8. To avoid sunburns, put some dog safe sun cream on their ears and noses. Especially if it's a white or light coloured dog.
  9. If you suspect your dog is suffering from heatstroke, contact the vet immediately. Signs include:
    1. Heavy panting
    2. Excessive drooling
    3. Lethargy
    4. Uncoordinated movements
    5. Vomiting
    6. Collapsing
  10. Keep up to date with their anti-flea and tick lotions, as both are more prevalent during the summer months.
